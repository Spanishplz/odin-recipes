CAuSA

Description

In the ancient Peru, it was prepared with yellow potatoes, which have a soft texture, and kneaded with crushed chilli peppers though it can be done with several other varieties of potatoes, being the papa amarilla (yellow potato) the most commonly used.There isn't a consensus about the origin of the name but the word quechua kawsay means food or what feeds, a name also given to the potato. It usually made with peppers, chilli pepper and avocado. It can be filled with chicken (like on this recipe), tuna, trout, shellfish or any other white meat.

Ingredients

  • For the Chicken
    • 1 large cooked chicken breast, diced or shredded
    • 1/4 cup green peass
    • 1/4 cup diced cooked carrot
    • 1 teaspoon minced shallot
    • 2 tablespoon finely chopped roasted red peppers
    • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1 teaspoon lime juice
    • 1/4 cup mayonnaise, or as needed
  • For the Potato Mixture
    • 1 1/4 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered
    • 2 tablespoons aji amarillo chili paste, or to taste
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 lime, juiced or more to taste
    • cayenne pepper to taste
    • salt to taste
    • 1 avocado, quartered and sliced
  • For the Sauce
    • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
    • 1 tablespoon sour cream (Optional)
    • 1 small clove garlic, crushed
    • 2 teaspoon aji amarillo chili paste, or to taste
    • 1 teaspoon water as needed

Steps

  1. Combine boiled chicken, green pease, carrots, shallot, roasted red peppers, cilantro and lime juice in a bowl.
  2. Season it with salt and cayenne. Add mayonnaise and mix until combined.
  3. Cover the mixture with a plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to use.
  4. Add aji amarillo, olive oil, and lime juice to the potatoes.
  5. Season with cayenne and salt. Mash together with the potato masher.
  6. Switch to a spatula and mix until completely smooth
  7. Line four 6-ounce ramekins with plastic wrap. Scoop mashed potatoes evenly into ramekins; press and smooth out the tops.
  8. Cover each with a layor of avocado slices.
  9. Fill ramekin to the top with chicken salad, pressing it down and pulling up the plastic wrap to eliminate any air pockets.
  10. Cover with final layer of mashed potatoes, going up to 1/2 inch or more over the rim.
  11. Seal top with a new layer of plastic wrap and fold the sides over to seal. Refrigerate salads until completely chilled, at least 1 hour.
  12. Mix mayonnaise, sour cream, garlic, and aji amarillo together for the sauce, adding a splash of water to adjust the thickness.
  13. Remove the top layer of plastic wrap from each salad.
  14. Invert each ramekin onto a serving plate: remove ramekiuns, then plastic wrap.
  15. Spoon some sauce around each salad and on top.
