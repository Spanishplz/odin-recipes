LOMO SALTADO

Description

Lomo saltado is a popular traditional Peruvian dish, a stir fry that typically combines marinated strips of sirloin (or other beef steak) with onions, tomatoes, french fries, and other ingredients: typically served with rice on the side. The dish originated as part of the chifa tradition, the Chinese cuisine of Peru, though its popularity has made it part of the mainstream culture.

The use of both and potatoes (which originated in Peru) and rice (which originated in Asia) as starches are typical of the cultural blending that the dish represents. It is considered one of the most loved dishes that shows the rich fusion of not only two cultures, but the old and the new.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds top sirloin steak
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • 16 ounces of french fries
  • 1 1/2 cups sliced red onion
  • 1 medium orange bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow chilli pepper (preferably a peruvian aji amarillo), sliced
  • 1 medium orange bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onion
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 3 large tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and slices into strips
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Sliced beef horizontally into 1/4-inch strips and transfer into mixing bowl.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of soy sauce and the sugar.
  3. Mix well and place in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour: longer is fine.
  4. Preheat an oven to 200 degrees. Line a sheet pan with a silicone liner.
  5. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large, nonstick skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add french fries and cook until golden and crisp, 5 to 7 minutes.
  7. Transfer to the prepared pan and place in preheat oven to keep warm
  8. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in the same skillet over high heat until the oil begins to smoke
  9. Sear the beef strips in 2 or 3 smaller batches, until browned but still pink in places, for 1 to 2 minutes per batch.
  10. Don't try to cook the meat all the way as it will finish cooking with the vegetables later
  11. Place the skillet back over high heat, and add ramaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil.
  12. Transfer in red onion, bell pepper, aji amarillo, red onion, along with a large pinch of salt.
  13. Cook, stirring, until the onions and peppers just begin to soften, 3 to 5 minutes.
  14. Add garlic and ginger to the pan: cook and stir for 1 minute.
  15. Add tomateo wedges and cook, stirring, until they begin to soften and start to release their juices.
  16. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, white vinegar and the browned beef into the pan, along with any accumulated juices and stir to combine.
  17. Stir cilantro and crispy, cooked french fries into the pan, tossing everything to combine.
  18. Taste for salt and adjust if needed.
  19. Serve immediately.
