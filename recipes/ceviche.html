CEvIChE

Description

One of the most famous dishes from Peru. Made from raw fish cured in fresh citrus juices, usually lemon or lime. It's also spiced with chili peppers and onions. It's name originates from the Quechuan word siwichi, which means fresh or tender fish.The peruvian origin of the dish is widely agreed upon, supported by several world renown chefs.

There are many variations of this dish around the world but in general most of them are marinated in a citrus-based mixture. The citric acid causes the proteins in the seafood to become denatured, appearing cooked. Most countries have given ceviche its own touch of individuality by adding their own particular garnishes.The following recipe is from Peru.

Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 small corn
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 cup of fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 stalk celery, sliced
  • 1/4 cup lightly packed cilantro leaves
  • 1 pinch ground cumin
  • 1 glove garlic, minced
  • 1 habanero pepper, seeded and minced
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • 1 pound fresh tilapia, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 pound medium shrimp - peeled, deveined, and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 lettuce, separated into leaves

Steps

  1. Place the potatoes and sweet potatoes in a saucepan and cover with water.
  2. Simmer until the sweet potatoes are easily pierced with a fork, then drain, and set aside to cool to room temperature.
  3. Cook the corn.
  4. Place the sliced onion in a bowl of warm water and let it stand 10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  5. Meanwhile place the lime juice, celery, cilantro and cumin into a bowl of a blender, and puree until smooth.
  6. Pour this mixture into a large glass bowl, and stir in the garlic and habanero pepper.
  7. Season with salt and pepper, then stir in the diced tilapia and shrimp.
  8. Set aside to marinate for an hour, stirring occasionally.
  9. The dish is done once it turns firm and opaque.
  10. To serve, peel the sweet potatoes and cut into slices.
  11. Stir the onions into the fish mixture.
  12. Line serving bowls with lettuce leaves and corn.
  13. Spoon the ceviche with its juice into the bowls and garnish with slices of potato.
